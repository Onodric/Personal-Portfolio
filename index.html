About Me

When my family moved to North Carolina, my new elementary school was a "magnet" school. All I cared about was that I got to draw and paint every day, and we had an hour to play around on an Apple II, typing out instructions to a triangular turtle using LOGO. Throughout my childhood, I was exposed to my father's civil engineering practice and the growing use of computers as tools in a professional workplace. As a family, we got our first home computer when I was 12: an IBM PS/2. It dominated my time: I programmed BASIC, learned to navigate Assembly, and joined numerous BBS's talking to far-flung people late into the night. That PS/2 was the last PC I didn't build myself. I encountered Unix, and quickly thereafter Linux, through NSCU and Georgia Tech.

As time went on, art became my focus, and I became more interested in making physical objects. I have been a cartographer, a civil engineering technician, a visual artist, a CAD operator, an actor, a set builder, and a welder. In 2016, after helping guide one daughter into college and welcoming another into Montessori school, I decided to change my hobby into a fulfilling career. A career with rewarding intellectual challenges, more opportunities, and above all interactions with great, smart people. Following my ever-present fascination with computers, I turned to NSS and software development. Every day I am challenged in a new way. I am surrounded by interesting and brilliant people. I work together with those people to solve problems I never dreamed I could solve. What's next?

leXicology is a free and open-source puzzle generator. This app uses a word list of 450,000 English terms to populate a crossword, along with the Wiktionry API to supply hints. The functionality of the unique crossword is a bit different than conventional paper and pencil puzzles. These crosswords are focussed on building lexicon, not on game completion. Clues are dictionary definitions, and users can see the answer at any point.
